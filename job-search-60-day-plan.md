## Quick Reference: STAR Method

**S - Situation**: Set the context (1-2 sentences)
**T - Task**: What was your responsibility? (1 sentence)
**A - Action**: What did YOU do? Be specific. (3-5 sentences)
**R - Result**: Quantify the outcome (1-2 sentences)

**Example:**
- S: "Our product had a 40% churn rate, threatening $2M ARR."
- T: "As PM, I was tasked with reducing churn by Q3."
- A: "I analyzed exit surveys, identified onboarding as the issue, redesigned the first-week experience, and ran A/B tests."
- R: "Churn dropped to 25% in 8 weeks, saving $800K ARR."

## Interview Questions Bank

### Behavioral
1. Tell me about a time you led a project
2. Describe a conflict with a teammate
3. Tell me about a failure
4. How do you prioritize?
5. Tell me about a time you influenced without authority
6. Describe a difficult decision you made
7. Tell me about a time you dealt with ambiguity
8. How do you handle tight deadlines?
9. Tell me about receiving critical feedback
10. Describe a time you went above and beyond

### Role/Company Fit
1. Why do you want to work here?
2. Why are you leaving your current role?
3. What interests you about this role?
4. Where do you see yourself in 5 years?
5. What's your management style?

### Your Turn to Ask
1. What does success look like in this role at 30/60/90 days?
2. What are the biggest challenges for this team right now?
3. How would you describe the team culture?
4. What's the growth path for this role?
5. Why did you join this company?
6. What's something you wish you knew before joining?
7. How does this team collaborate with [other team]?
8. What are the company's priorities for this year?
